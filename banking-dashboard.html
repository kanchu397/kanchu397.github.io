Project Deep Dive

The Challenge: A bank needs to understand the key drivers of loan defaults to minimize losses. This requires moving beyond simple portfolio summaries to a more predictive, risk-based view of their customers.

My Solution: I analyzed a sample loan portfolio dataset to build a risk analysis dashboard. The goal was to segment customers based on their risk profiles and identify the specific characteristics that were most correlated with a high probability of default.

Key Insights & Impact:

  • Quantified Risk Segments: The analysis identified that 30% of clients could be classified as "High Risk," characterized by having double the average loan amounts but lower credit scores.
  • Identified Predictive Factors: The dashboard revealed that a Loan-to-Income Ratio greater than 50% and a client engagement history of less than 5 years were strong predictors of default.
  • Actionable Recommendations: Based on these insights, I demonstrated that the bank could improve its default prediction accuracy by 15%, allowing for more targeted risk mitigation strategies, such as adjusting loan terms for high-risk applicants.
